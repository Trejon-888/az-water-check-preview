My Story

15+ Years Spent Asking One Simple Question

I started in residential water treatment over 15 years ago, when I knocked on my first door and asked a homeowner if she'd ever tested her tap water. She hadn't. Most people haven't. That single conversation changed the way I thought about my work.

For more than a decade, I worked with several leading water treatment companies across the Southwest. I tested thousands of homes. I sat at thousands of kitchen tables. I watched parents look at their water test strip and go quiet. That moment never gets old. It's the moment they realize the water they trusted with their kids isn't what they thought it was.

Eventually, I founded Aquafeel Solutions Arizona because I wanted to do this work my way: no high-pressure pitches, no scare tactics, no scripts. Just curiosity, honesty, and the best system I could find for Arizona's specific water problems.

Today I'm the only authorized Aquafeel Solutions dealer in the state of Arizona. I've personally certified and overseen hundreds of dual-tank installations across Phoenix, Mesa, Chandler, Gilbert, Tempe, Scottsdale, Peoria, Glendale, Tucson, and beyond. And I still test the water at every home myself.

Why Arizona

Why I Became Arizona-Focused

I could have stayed generalist. The water industry is everywhere. But after years of testing homes across multiple states, I noticed something: Arizona is different.

The Phoenix metro averages 15-25 grains per gallon of hardness. That's more than double what the Water Quality Association calls "very hard." On top of that, our water carries chloramine (not just chlorine), and the heat amplifies how it interacts with skin, hair, and home plumbing.

Generic water systems aren't built for this. A standard softener doesn't solve the chemical side. A standard filter doesn't solve the hardness side. Arizona families need a system that does both. That's why I focused here, and why I chose the dual-tank approach.

Why Aquafeel

Why I Chose Dual-Tank, Not Single

For most of my career, I worked with single-tank softeners. They're the industry standard. But over the years I kept seeing the same problem: a softener removes hardness, but it doesn't touch chemicals. A filter removes chemicals, but it doesn't soften. Families ended up needing two systems, two installations, two sets of maintenance.

When I discovered the Aquafeel dual-tank platform, it solved that. Tank 1 handles minerals and conditioning. Tank 2 uses catalytic carbon to remove chlorine, chloramine, heavy metals, and disinfection byproducts. One system. One install. And the only one I'd put in my own home.
