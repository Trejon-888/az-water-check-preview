Why Glendale Water is Different

Glendale sits in the West Valley with more than 250,000 residents stretched across neighborhoods that include the master-planned Arrowhead Ranch, the entertainment hub of Westgate, and the historic core around Historic Downtown Glendale. City of Glendale Water Services delivers water across the city's nearly 60 square miles by blending SRP surface water, CAP Colorado River water, and groundwater from local wells. The mix changes by zone and by season, which is part of why Glendale homeowners sometimes notice their water tastes different than what they remember from a previous home elsewhere in the Valley.

Glendale is one of the few Valley cities to actively use all three major water sources: SRP, CAP, and groundwater. City of Glendale Water Services operates the Cholla Water Treatment Plant and the Pyramid Peak Water Treatment Plant to process surface water, and runs a network of wells that supplement the system during peak demand. That diversification gives Glendale resilience against drought and infrastructure disruptions, but it also means your block can see meaningful changes in water chemistry depending on which sources the city is drawing from. Customers in Arrowhead Ranch on the north side of the city often see different mineral readings than residents downtown near Historic Downtown Glendale, because the pressure zones and pump stations feeding those areas pull from different treatment facilities. Hardness across the city sits in the 17 to 22 GPG range, with chloramine used as the residual disinfectant. Customers near State Farm Stadium also report taste changes during major event days when water demand spikes and the system shifts more aggressively to groundwater backup.

Glendale Water Test FAQ

Is Glendale tap water safe to drink?

Yes. Glendale tap water meets all EPA Safe Drinking Water Act standards. City of Glendale Water Services publishes annual Consumer Confidence Reports and operates two modern treatment plants. The water is safe to drink even though hardness and chloramine remain present.

Where does Glendale get its tap water?

Glendale draws from three sources: the Salt River Project surface water system, the Central Arizona Project Colorado River canal, and a network of municipal groundwater wells. Surface water is treated at the Cholla and Pyramid Peak plants.

How hard is the water in Glendale?

Glendale water typically tests between 17 and 22 grains per gallon, well into the very hard category. Hardness can vary by 2 to 3 GPG depending on your zone and the season.

Why does my downtown Glendale home have rust-colored water some mornings?

Older neighborhoods near Historic Downtown Glendale sometimes have aged galvanized supply lines that react with mineral-rich water over time. The discoloration usually clears after running the tap for 30 seconds, but if it persists, a whole-home filter with sediment-stage prefiltration solves the issue.

Does Glendale add fluoride to its water?

Yes. Glendale fluoridates municipal water at the optimal level set by Arizona health authorities. Reverse osmosis at the kitchen tap is the standard removal method if you prefer fluoride-free drinking water.

Can you test water in Arrowhead Ranch?

Yes. We test water in Arrowhead Ranch, Westgate, Sierra Verde, Marbella Vineyards, Stonebrook, and every other Glendale neighborhood. Free in-home testing takes about 15 minutes.

Will major events at State Farm Stadium affect my home water?

Generally no, but during the very largest events the system can shift toward groundwater backup, which sometimes produces minor taste changes. Routine residential water quality is not impacted by stadium operations.
