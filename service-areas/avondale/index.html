Why Avondale Water is Different

Avondale sits in the heart of the West Valley, sharing borders with Goodyear, Glendale, and Phoenix, and serving more than 90,000 residents through City of Avondale Water. The city blends groundwater pulled from beneath the Phoenix Raceway basin with CAP Colorado River water, producing some of the hardest tap water readings in the metro. From the established neighborhoods of Garden Lakes and Coldwater Springs to the newer master-planned developments along Donatela, every Avondale home draws from the same demanding water environment.

Avondale relies more heavily on groundwater than most Phoenix metro cities. The aquifer beneath the West Valley is mineral-rich, and water that has spent millennia in those formations arrives at the tap carrying calcium, magnesium, iron, and trace elements that push hardness readings to 22 or even 24 GPG during peak summer pumping. City of Avondale Water blends groundwater with CAP Colorado River water to manage taste, supply, and treatment costs, but the groundwater contribution is significant year-round. The city uses chloramine as the residual disinfectant to keep water safe across the distribution network. Customers in Garden Lakes and Rancho Santa Fe often see different mineral readings than residents in Crystal Gardens or Donatela depending on which wells and CAP delivery points are feeding their pressure zone on a given day.

Avondale Water Test FAQ

Is Avondale tap water safe to drink?

Yes. Avondale tap water meets all EPA Safe Drinking Water Act standards. City of Avondale Water publishes annual Consumer Confidence Reports and actively manages a blend of groundwater and surface water to maintain quality. Compliance does not address hardness or chloramine.

Where does Avondale get its tap water?

Avondale sources water primarily from local groundwater wells supplemented by Central Arizona Project Colorado River water. The blend favors groundwater more than most Phoenix metro cities.

How hard is the water in Avondale?

Avondale water typically tests between 18 and 24 grains per gallon, putting it at the upper end of the very hard category. Summer peak readings can hit 24 GPG when groundwater contribution is highest.

Why does my Avondale home have so much orange staining?

Higher iron content from local groundwater wells produces orange staining around irrigation heads, hose bibs, and sometimes inside toilet tanks. Whole-home filtration with iron-specific media solves this issue at the source.

Does Avondale add fluoride to its water?

Yes. Avondale fluoridates municipal water at the level set by Arizona health authorities. Standard softeners do not remove fluoride; reverse osmosis at the kitchen tap does.

Can you test water in Garden Lakes or Rancho Santa Fe?

Yes. We test water in Garden Lakes, Rancho Santa Fe, Coldwater Springs, Crystal Gardens, Donatela, and every other Avondale neighborhood. Free in-home testing takes about 15 minutes.

Why does my dishwasher film over so quickly here?

With Avondale hardness running as high as 24 GPG, dissolved minerals deposit on dishes and glassware every cycle. Even with rinse aid, the white film comes back. Whole-home softening or filtration is the only durable fix.
