Arizona Water Hardness Levels

Why Phoenix Metro Has Some of the Hardest Water in America

Here in Arizona, especially in Phoenix Metro, the average water hardness is among the highest in the United States. Many homes measure between 15-25 grains per gallon, far exceeding the 7+ threshold that the Water Quality Association classifies as "very hard." This means your water is loaded with dissolved minerals, chemicals, and sediments that affect everything from your skin to your pipes.

10-40

Extremely Hard

Common in Phoenix, Mesa, Chandler, Gilbert, Scottsdale, Glendale, Peoria

  • Chalky white residue on faucets and showerheads
  • Dry, itchy skin after showering
  • Soap and shampoo won't lather properly
  • Cloudy buildup inside pipes and water heater
  • Clogged fixtures and reduced water pressure
  • Stiff, rough-feeling clothes after washing
  • Spotted dishes and glassware
  • Higher energy bills from scale buildup
50-80

Moderately Hard

Tucson, Flagstaff, some Northern AZ regions

  • Some mineral spotting on dishes
  • Occasional dry skin symptoms
  • Mild soap efficiency reduction
  • Gradual buildup in water heater
  • Slight impact on appliance lifespan
90-100

Soft Water

Rare in Arizona -- mostly found in homes with filtration systems

  • Smooth, hydrated skin and hair
  • Soap lathers easily, less product needed
  • Spotless dishes and glassware
  • Extended appliance lifespan
  • Lower energy bills
  • Cleaner, softer laundry
